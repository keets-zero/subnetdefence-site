The game

Threats follow the lit path toward your core. You build towers to stop them — and every wave gets harder. Runs are endless, but they always end: the wall catches up eventually. How deep you get is the score.

Between runs you spend what you earned in the Lab, making the next run reach further. Bosses are drawn from real malware, described technically — Morris Worm, Melissa, ILOVEYOU — with no country or attribution attached. The glitches you fight are real attack techniques.

Eight towers

Firewall, Chain, Honeypot, ICE Cannon, Zero-Day, Rootkit, Deep Probe, Crypto Miner.

Real threats

Bosses and glitches based on actual malware and attack techniques.

Endless runs

Five difficulty tracks. The exponential wall means every run ends.

Privacy policy

Last updated: 18 July 2026

Subnet Defence is developed by Keets Project (Stefan Ekström), Sweden. This policy explains what the app does and does not do with your information.

What we collect

Nothing. The app does not collect, transmit, or store any personal information on our servers. There is no account, no login, and no tracking.

Data stored on your device

The game saves your progress locally on your device — your cores, upgrades, best waves, and settings. This data never leaves your phone. It is removed when you uninstall the app, and can be cleared at any time from the in-game settings ("Reset progress").

Reporting a bug

If you use the "Report a bug" button, the app opens your own email app with a pre-filled message containing the app version and your device model. Nothing is sent unless you choose to send it, and the message goes to us only if you press send.

Children

The app is not directed at children and collects no information from anyone, regardless of age.

Changes

Future versions may add optional advertising or in-app purchases. If that happens, this policy will be updated before those features ship, and the change will be described here.
